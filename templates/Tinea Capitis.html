TINEA CAPITIS

Tinea Capitis is a fungal infection of the scalp that causes hair loss, itching, and flaky, scaly patches. It is more common in children but can affect adults as well.

What Causes It?

  • Fungal Infection: Tinea Capitis is caused by fungi, commonly dermatophytes, that infect the hair follicles and scalp.
  • Person-to-Person Contact: The infection can spread through direct contact with an infected person.
  • Contaminated Objects: Sharing combs, hats, or other personal items can spread the infection.
  • Weakened Immune System: People with compromised immune systems may be more susceptible to the infection.

How Can It Be Treated?

Tinea Capitis can be treated with antifungal medications:

  • Oral Antifungal Medications: Prescription antifungal drugs, such as griseofulvin or terbinafine, are commonly used to treat Tinea Capitis.
  • Topical Antifungal Creams: While they may not be sufficient for scalp infections, antifungal shampoos can help reduce the fungal load.
  • Keep the Scalp Clean: Regularly wash the scalp and hair with antifungal shampoo to prevent spreading.
  • Avoid Sharing Personal Items: To prevent the spread, avoid sharing combs, hats, and towels with others.
