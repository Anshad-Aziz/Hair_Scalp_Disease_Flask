FOLLICULITIS

Folliculitis happens when hair follicles get inflamed, often from infection or irritation. It looks like small red bumps or pimples around the hair follicles and can feel itchy or painful.

What Causes It?

  • Bacteria: The most common cause is bacteria like Staphylococcus aureus.
  • Fungal Infections: Fungi can also infect hair follicles and cause inflammation.
  • Friction: Tight clothing or shaving can irritate the skin and lead to folliculitis.
  • Blocked Follicles: Oils, sweat, or hair products can clog follicles and cause infection.

How Can It Be Treated?

You can manage Folliculitis with these steps:

  • Good Hygiene: Keep the area clean and dry to avoid infection.
  • Warm Compress: Use a warm, moist cloth to reduce swelling and relieve discomfort.
  • Topical Treatments: Apply antibiotic or antifungal creams to clear up the infection.
  • Avoid Irritation: Wear loose clothing and avoid shaving the affected area.
  • See a Doctor: For severe or recurring cases, a doctor may prescribe stronger medications or other treatments.
