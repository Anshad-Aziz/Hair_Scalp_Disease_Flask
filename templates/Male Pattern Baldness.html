MALE PATTERN BALDNESS

Male Pattern Baldness, also known as androgenetic alopecia, is a common condition where men experience hair loss, usually starting at the hairline or the crown of the head. It is a gradual process and typically runs in families.

What Causes It?

  • Genetics: It often runs in families and is inherited.
  • Hormones: Changes in hormones, particularly dihydrotestosterone (DHT), affect hair growth.
  • Age: Hair loss becomes more common as men get older.

How Can It Be Treated?

There are ways to manage Male Pattern Baldness:

  • Minoxidil: A topical solution applied to the scalp to slow hair loss and promote growth.
  • Finasteride: A prescription medication that blocks DHT to prevent hair loss.
  • Hair Transplant: A surgical procedure where hair follicles are moved to bald areas.
  • Low-Level Laser Therapy: Uses light therapy to stimulate hair growth.
  • Hairstyling: Using creative styles or wigs to cover thinning areas.
