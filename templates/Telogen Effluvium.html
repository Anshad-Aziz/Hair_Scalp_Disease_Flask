TELOGEN EFFLUVIUM

Telogen Effluvium is a type of hair loss that happens when a large number of hair follicles enter the resting (telogen) phase of the hair cycle, causing shedding. It is often triggered by physical or emotional stress, illness, or other factors.

What Causes It?

  • Stress: Emotional or physical stress can cause hair to fall out prematurely.
  • Illness: Fever, surgery, or an infection can lead to temporary hair loss.
  • Medications: Certain drugs, such as chemotherapy or birth control pills, can cause hair loss.
  • Hormonal Changes: Changes in hormones, especially after pregnancy or childbirth, can trigger Telogen Effluvium.
  • Dietary Deficiencies: Lack of essential nutrients like iron or protein can contribute to hair shedding.

How Can It Be Treated?

Telogen Effluvium is usually temporary, and the hair often regrows over time. Here are ways to help manage it:

  • Address Underlying Causes: Identifying and managing triggers like stress or medication can help prevent further hair loss.
  • Healthy Diet: Ensure you are getting enough vitamins and minerals, such as iron, vitamin D, and biotin.
  • Minoxidil: A topical solution that can help stimulate hair growth and prevent further shedding.
  • Stress Management: Practicing relaxation techniques, like meditation or yoga, can reduce stress levels and improve hair health.
  • Avoid Hair Damage: Avoid harsh treatments or excessive heat styling to minimize hair breakage.
