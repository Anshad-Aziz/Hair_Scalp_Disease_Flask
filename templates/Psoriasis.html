PSORIASIS

Psoriasis is a chronic autoimmune condition that causes skin cells to grow too quickly, leading to thick, scaly patches on the skin. It can affect different areas of the body, including the scalp.

What Causes It?

  • Immune System: Psoriasis occurs when the immune system mistakenly attacks healthy skin cells.
  • Genetics: It often runs in families and can be inherited.
  • Triggers: Stress, infections, certain medications, or injuries can trigger flare-ups.

How Can It Be Treated?

You can manage Psoriasis with these steps:

  • Topical Treatments: Creams or ointments, like corticosteroids, to reduce inflammation.
  • Phototherapy: Exposure to UV light can slow down the growth of skin cells.
  • Oral Medications: Medications to suppress the immune system and reduce symptoms.
  • Biologic Drugs: Targeted treatments that can help control severe psoriasis.
  • Moisturizers: Keep the skin hydrated to prevent dryness and itching.
