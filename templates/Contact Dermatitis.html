Contact Dermatitis

Contact Dermatitis is when your skin gets red, itchy, and inflamed after touching something irritating or an allergen. It can be uncomfortable but is usually not serious.

What Causes It?

  • Irritants: Soaps, detergents, or chemicals that hurt your skin.
  • Allergens: Things like nickel in jewelry, latex, or plants that cause an allergic reaction.
  • Weather: Very hot or cold weather can irritate your skin.

How Can It Be Treated?

You can manage Contact Dermatitis with these steps:

  • Avoid Triggers: Stay away from things that irritate your skin.
  • Moisturizers: Use creams to keep your skin soft and help it heal.
  • Anti-Itch Creams: Apply creams to reduce itching and redness.
  • Antihistamines: Take medicines to stop allergic reactions and relieve itching.
  • Cool Cloths: Use a damp cloth to calm irritated skin.
