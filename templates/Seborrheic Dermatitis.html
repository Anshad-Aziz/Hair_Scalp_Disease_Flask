SEBORRHEIC DERMATITIS

Seborrheic Dermatitis is a common skin condition that causes a red, itchy rash with white or yellow scales. It often affects areas of the skin where sebaceous (oil) glands are most active, like the scalp, face, and upper chest.

What Causes It?

  • Yeast Growth: A type of yeast (Malassezia) that grows on the skin can trigger the condition.
  • Genetics: It can run in families, making some people more prone to developing it.
  • Stress: Stress can sometimes make the condition worse.
  • Other Conditions: People with conditions like Parkinson’s disease or HIV are more likely to develop Seborrheic Dermatitis.

How Can It Be Treated?

These treatments can help manage Seborrheic Dermatitis:

  • Medicated Shampoos: Shampoos with antifungal ingredients like ketoconazole or selenium sulfide can reduce yeast growth on the scalp.
  • Topical Creams: Anti-inflammatory creams or lotions, such as corticosteroids, can reduce redness and itching.
  • Moisturizers: Keeping the skin hydrated helps reduce dryness and irritation.
  • Antifungal Medications: Oral antifungals may be prescribed for severe cases.
  • Avoid Triggers: Identify and avoid triggers such as stress or harsh skin products.

IF THE PROBLEM DOESN’T GO AWAY, TALK TO A DOCTOR.
